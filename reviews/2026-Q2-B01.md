## 2. What to continue

- **Squat programming is dialed.** Low-bar squat tracked its plan almost exactly and is your
  most reliable lift — it responds to the top-set + backoff structure. Keep it.
- **Bench-variant approach for shoulder management.** Running CGB / Paused Larsen instead of
  forcing comp bench was the right instinct given the shoulder.
- **Sumo strength is real and ahead of where the plan expected.** Keep the paused-RDL +
  paused-sumo accessory work feeding it.
- **You logged honestly** — including the athlete notes about fear and pain. That candor is
  what makes these reviews possible. Don't lose it.

## 3. What to improve / change

- **Systemic RPE overshoot — the defining failure of this block.** It wasn't one lift, it was
  the pattern: Dip +70 @9 (plan @6–7), OHP 120 @9.5 (plan @7), CGB 215 @9 (plan @7–7.5),
  Larsen 205 @9 (plan said *do not exceed 7*), Sumo 455 @9. You consistently chased the
  number on the bar instead of the RPE on the card.
- **The cost was real:** the W4 CGB session was "traumatising," you had a fear-of-failure rep
  you didn't complete, and the shoulder flared. That's the downstream of grinding a structural
  injury — exactly the thing to never do again.
- **Accessory logging had gaps** (several blanks / #VALUE! in the sheet). Hard to autoregulate
  what isn't recorded. (Now solved by the Hevy sync — this is automatic going forward.)

## Coach's notes — power & health

- **Power:** the engine is strong; the limiter this block was *control*, not capacity. A lifter
  who can pull 455 sumo and dip +80×8 but rates them @9 doesn't need more intensity — he needs
  to leave reps in the tank so the next session isn't compromised. Strength is built on the sets
  you *didn't* grind.
- **Health:** the shoulder told you the truth in W4 and you pushed anyway. The single most
  power-preserving habit you can build is stopping the set at the cap — it's not caution, it's
  what keeps you training for the next decade instead of rehabbing for the next year.
