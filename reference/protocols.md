## Deload Policy

### Block structure
- **Block length**: 5 weeks
- **W1 — Calibration**: RPE cap 7, 60–70% of top working weights. Acts as transitional
  deload from previous block's W4 peak. Volume moderate. Groove form.
- **W2 — Establish**: RPE 7–8 on main lifts. Volume up to full working level. Same
  accessory loads as W1.
- **W3 — Push**: RPE 8–8.5. Accessory loads bump (2-week cycle rolls over).
- **W4 — Overreach**: RPE 8.5–9.5. Top sets or heavy triples. PRs possible. Highest
  fatigue point.
- **W5 — Deload**: RPE cap 6.5–7. Volume ~50%. Same movements, light loads. Rest the
  CNS before the next block's W1.

### Accessory progression inside a block
- **2-week cycles, not weekly.** W1+W2 share accessory loads. W3+W4 share a slightly
  heavier load (~5–10 lb on bar movements, 1 rep or 2.5 lb on machines). W5 drops to
  W1 loads or lighter.
- Rationale: weekly jumps are noise. 2 weeks shows whether a load is stable. Also makes
  logging trivial — "same as last week" is an acceptable entry.

### Full deload blocks
With W5 baked into every block, a separate full deload block is only called if fatigue
persists past W5 into the next W1. Triggers:
- 2 consecutive blocks ending with "cooked" self-report
- Shoulder flag rate >30% across a block
- RPE creep carrying from one block's W4 into the next block's W1
- Missing planned reps on main lifts in 2+ sessions of a new block's W1

If triggered: all lifts 60–70% of current 1RM, volume ~50%, RPE cap 7 across the board,
1 week minimum, up to 10 days. Skill work prioritized.

### Early deload triggers within a block (override schedule)
Drop the current week to W5-style loads if ANY of:
- RPE creep: same weight feeling 1+ RPE harder for 3 sessions
- Sleep "Poor" 3 sessions in a row
- Shoulder logs "Noticeable" 2+ sessions
- Missing planned reps on main lifts 2 sessions in a row
- User reports feeling cooked

### Why this structure
37 years old + 11 years training age + chronic shoulder = higher recovery demand. A
built-in W5 deload every 5 weeks is cheaper than a blown-up block. W1-as-calibration
keeps momentum while decompressing from the prior W4. 5 weeks (vs 4) gives one more
true working week (W3) between Calibration and Overreach.

## Triage Rules (3/2-day weeks, illness, travel)

### Default
4 training days per week. ~98% of weeks.

### 3 days (busy week)
Acceptable. Keep exposure to all 5 primary movement patterns across the week.
- **Day A**: Squat + Bench (primaries) + 2 accessories
- **Day B**: Sumo DL + Weighted Pull-up (primaries) + 1 upper accessory
- **Day C**: Weighted Dip + Bench volume OR Squat accessory + 2 accessories

Drop accessory volume first. Do not drop primaries. Keep planned RPE targets.
(Block 2 note: with dips removed, current-block.md's own 3-day fallback — D1, D2, D4 —
takes precedence over this generic pattern.)

### 2 days (rare; travel, e.g. Korea trip)
Consolidate hard. Longer sessions OK (~90 min).
- **Day A**: Squat + Bench + Weighted Pull-up + 1 accessory
- **Day B**: Sumo DL + Weighted Dip + 1 back accessory + 1 arm/shoulder accessory

Reduce volume 30–40%. Hold intensity where possible. Skip accessories that aren't
earning their keep.

### Fever / acute illness
Do not train. Rest. Resume after 48 hours fever-free + normal energy, with a W1-style
intro session regardless of where you were in the block.

### Stomach issues (flagged as the realistic scenario)
If GI distress limits to 2 days, use the 2-day pattern. If weak or dehydrated, cut
intensity 10–15%. Fluids + electrolytes non-negotiable.

### Travel without a gym
Flag it. Coach writes a 2–3 day bodyweight/minimalist version (backpack pull-ups, chair
dips, split squats, push-up variations, hollow body). Preserve movement patterns, not
1RM volume.

## Testing Protocol (1RM verification)

### When to test
- **Block W4 heavy singles**: soft test — top single at RPE 9–9.5 gives an e-1RM
  datapoint. Logged to Maxes, Source = `e-1RM`.
- **End of quarter (every 3 blocks)**: optional real 1RM test day, one lift at a time
  over 1–2 sessions. Source = `Gym Test`.
- **Meet / mock meet**: once or twice a year. Source = `Meet`.

### How to run a gym test
- One lift per session. Never two primaries same day.
- Test AFTER a deload (W1 of a new block, or after a full deload block).
- Warm-up per Warmup Standards.
- Attempts: RPE 8 single → RPE 9 single → small PR attempt only if the RPE 9 moved
  clean. Stop if form breaks.
- Do not grind. A grind that injures you erases 3 months of work.

### What counts
- **1RM**: clean rep, full ROM, no spotter assist beyond safety.
- **Pull-up**: chin clearly over bar, dead-hang start.
- **Dip**: full ROM at pain-free depth, full lockout, no kip.

### e-1RM formula
Epley: `weight × (1 + reps/30)` for 1–5 reps at RPE 9–10. Don't bother above 5 reps.

## Warmup Standards

### General (every session, before first primary)
5–8 min: 3 min easy cardio + dynamic work (leg swings, hip circles, thoracic rotations,
band pull-aparts x15, face pulls x15) + lift-specific ramp below.

### Squat
Bar x10 → 40% x5 → 60% x3 → 70% x2 → 80% x1 → working sets.

### Bench (shoulder priority)
Before any bar work: 2x15 band pull-aparts + 10 scap push-ups + 10 face pulls. Then:
Bar x10 → 40% x5 → 60% x3 → 75% x1 → 85% x1 (if top set is heavy) → working sets.

### Sumo DL
Don't over-warm-up — DL fatigues fast.
Bar x8 → 40% x5 → 60% x3 → 75% x1 → working sets.
Always 1–2 sets hip airplane / 90-90 hip mobility before sumo.

### Weighted pull-up
BW x5 → BW+10 kg x3 → BW + 50% of working load x2 → BW + 80% x1 → working sets.

### Weighted dip (shoulder-first)
Band pull-aparts x15 → scap dips x5 → BW dips x5 → +20% x3 → +50% x2 → +80% x1 →
working sets.

### Don't skip
The shoulder warmup before bench/dip is non-negotiable. Cheap insurance.

## Accessory Selection (weak point → accessory mapping)

> Every accessory traces back to: weak point → target muscle/position → exercise →
> expected carryover.

### Squat — no major weak points; accessories for work capacity
| Weak point | Target | Exercise options |
|---|---|---|
| Generic leg mass | Quads, glutes | Leg press, Bulgarian split squat, walking lunge |
| Upper back for bar position | Upper back, rear delts | Chest-supported row, face pulls |
| Hip drive | Glutes, posterior chain | Hip thrust, RDL |

### Bench — shoulder pain, unstable form, loses arch
| Weak point | Target | Exercise options |
|---|---|---|
| Loses arch under fatigue | Upper back tightness, lat engagement | Chest-supported row, pullovers, DB row |
| Lockout weakness | Triceps | Close-grip bench, JM press, overhead triceps ext |
| Off-chest weakness | Pecs, stretch position | Paused bench, deep DB bench, floor press (shoulder-friendly) |
| Shoulder health | Rear delts, rotator cuff | Face pulls, band pull-aparts, external rotation |

If shoulder flaring: substitute floor press or slingshot bench for comp bench for 1–2
sessions. See Shoulder Protocol.

### Sumo Deadlift — hitch after knees (lockout)
| Weak point | Target | Exercise options |
|---|---|---|
| Lockout hitch | Glutes, erectors, upper back | Rack pulls (above knee), hip thrust, GHR, back extensions |
| Off-floor weakness | Hams, quads, start position | Deficit sumo, pause-at-knee sumo |
| Upper back rounding | Traps, erectors | Chest-supported row, back extension, Yates row |

### Weighted Pull-up — chin doesn't clear at heavy weight
| Weak point | Target | Exercise options |
|---|---|---|
| Top ROM / chin clearance | Upper traps, rear delts, finishing pull | High-to-neck row, chest-to-bar pull-ups, band-assisted top-range |
| Lat width / pulling mass | Lats | Wide-grip pulldown, pullovers, bent-over row |
| Grip | Forearms | Dead hangs, farmer carry, thick grip |

### Weighted Dip — shoulder discomfort at bottom
| Weak point | Target | Exercise options |
|---|---|---|
| Shoulder discomfort at bottom | Anterior delt health, upper back | Limited-ROM dips, close-grip bench, floor press |
| Lockout | Triceps | Close-grip bench, overhead triceps, pushdowns |
| Chest depth | Pecs | DB bench, incline DB press |

Dip volume modulates with shoulder status — see Shoulder Protocol.

## Block Design Rules — Exercise Selection & Rotation

> What stays fixed and what rotates when a new block is designed. Applies to every
> block from Block 2 onward. (Condensed tier rule also lives in brain/memory.md.)

### Tier 1 — Primary movements (FIXED)
Never change. Same lift, competition style. Only load/sets/reps/RPE schema changes.
D1 Low-bar Squat · D2 Weighted Pull-up · D3 bench variant (see Tier 2) · D4 Sumo
Deadlift. D1 also carries a bench variant; D3 carries a squat accessory.

### Tier 2 — Secondary movements (ROTATE AT BLOCK BOUNDARIES, never mid-block)
- **Bench**: CGB, Spoto Press, Paused Larsen, comp bench w/ pause (when shoulder allows).
  **Max 2 bench variants per block** (note: Block 2 runs 3 — known deviation, user call).
- **Squat**: Paused low-bar, Tempo (3s ecc), High-bar.
- **Deadlift**: Paused RDL, Deficit sumo, Tempo pull.
- **Pull-up**: Lat pulldown, Weighted chin-up (supinated).

Pick based on current weak-point priority.

### Tier 3 — Accessories (PARTIAL ROTATION)
Keep 1–2 staples per category for a baseline; rotate the rest. Staple → rotate pool:

| Category | Staple | Rotate |
|---|---|---|
| Horizontal pull | Iso-Lateral or Meadows Row | Cable row, chest-supported row, DB row |
| Vertical pull | Lat Pulldown | Cable lat pullover, straight-arm pulldown, banded pulldown |
| Posterior chain | Weighted Back Extension | GHR, good morning, RDL (if not a secondary) |
| Shoulder health | Face Pull, Band Pull-Apart | Rear delt fly, Y-T-W, external rotation |
| Tricep | Tricep Pushdown | Overhead extension, JM press, dips (when shoulder allows) |
| Bicep / elbow | Cable Curl | Incline DB curl, hammer curl (if elbow tolerates), spider curl |
| Quad | Leg Extension (3s ecc) | Hack squat, Bulgarian split squat, leg press |
| Hamstring / hip | Seated Leg Curl | Nordic curl, lying leg curl, hip abduction |

### Rules summary
1. Primaries never change — only periodization schema.
2. Secondaries rotate at block boundaries only. Max 2 bench variants per block.
3. Accessories rotate partially — staples stay.
4. Every rotation must tie to a weak point. No variety for variety's sake.
5. Logging discipline applies to accessories. No silent skips.

## Shoulder Protocol

> The chronic one. Bench, dip, and pull-up all load the shoulder. Guides substitution
> and return-to-lift. Current status/restrictions: `brain/active-issues.md`.

### Session flag thresholds
| Status logged | Action |
|---|---|
| Fine | Normal programming |
| Slight | Proceed with extended warmup + RPE cap 8 on bench/dip. Note the session. |
| Noticeable (single session) | Reduce bench/dip intensity 5%, extended warmup. Monitor. |
| Noticeable (2+ sessions) | Substitute movements (below). Incident log entry. |
| Pain | Substitute immediately. Incident log entry. Consider 3–7 day rest for the pattern. |

### Substitutions
**Bench →** 1. Floor press (first choice — removes bottom-range stress) · 2. Slingshot
bench · 3. Paused bench 1–2" off chest · 4. Spoto press · 5. Close-grip bench.
**Dip →** 1. Limited-ROM dips (stop above pain threshold) · 2. Close-grip bench ·
3. Weighted push-ups · 4. DB floor press.
**Pull-up →** 1. Neutral-grip pull-up · 2. Reduce added load 15–20% · 3. Pulldown
machine.

### Return to full lifts (after 2+ Noticeable or any Pain session)
- **Days 1–3**: pattern rest (substitute). Everything else as programmed.
- **Session 1 back**: RPE 6–7, ~70% of pre-flare weight. Form focus, controlled eccentric.
- **Session 2**: RPE 7–8, ~80%.
- **Session 3**: RPE 8, ~90%.
- **Session 4**: resume planned programming.
- Any "Noticeable"+ during the return → restart at session 1.

### Warmup (non-negotiable before any pressing)
Band pull-aparts 2x15 · Face pulls 2x15 · Scap push-ups 2x10 · Scap dips (BW) 1x5 ·
External rotation (light DB/band) 2x10 each side.

### Daily home rehab routine (carry-over from Block 1)
Run daily, training and non-training days:
Band internal rotation 2x15/side · Band external rotation 2x15/side · Band pull-apart
2x15 · Overhead band pull-apart 2x12 · Face pull (band) 2x15.
If it takes more than 8 minutes, you're overdoing it. Maintenance, not a workout.

### Red flags → medical professional
Night pain waking you · weakness (can't raise arm / grip collapses) · radiating
numbness/tingling · no improvement after 7 days of modified programming + rest.
Not a substitute for medical advice.

## Lower Back Protocol (placeholder — no active issue)

### Session flag thresholds
| Status | Action |
|---|---|
| Slight tightness | Extended hip/back warmup, proceed. |
| Noticeable pain mid-session | Stop the lift. Accessories or end session. |
| Sharp pain on a rep | Stop immediately. Full session rest for that pattern. |

### Common triggers
Lumbar rounding on heavy sumo (most likely) · bracing breakdown under heavy squat ·
fatigue-driven form breakdown.

### Substitutions
**Sumo DL →** Block pulls (mid-shin) · rack pull (above knee) · trap bar DL · hip thrust.
**Squat →** Box squat · safety bar squat · leg press (last resort).

### Return progression
Same as shoulder: 70% → 80% → 90% → full over 4 sessions. Do NOT rush back to heavy DL.

### Red flags → medical
Radiating leg pain · numbness/tingling in legs/feet · bladder/bowel changes (ER, now) ·
unable to stand upright after 24h · no improvement after 10 days.
