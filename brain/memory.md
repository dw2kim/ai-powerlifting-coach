## Known tendencies / recurring notes
- Shoulder is the chronic bottleneck. Constrains bench and dip. Monitor closely.
- Bench form: loses arch under fatigue — flag when RPE creeps.
- Sumo DL: hitches above knees when heavy — cue tall at lockout, tight bar path.

- Dip: shoulder discomfort at bottom — limit ROM when shoulder is cranky, don't skip.
- Squat: no form issues; underestimates self, pushes past prescribed RPE. Watch overreach.
- **Logging discipline**: accessories get skipped when the scheme is complex. Simpler > cleverer.

## Exercise tier rotation rule (2026-04-20)
- **Tier 1 — Primary** (never rotate): Low-bar Squat, Bench (or current proxy), Sumo DL,
  Weighted Pull-up, Weighted Dip. Same movement every block; only load/rep/RPE changes.
- **Tier 2 — Secondary** (rotate between blocks): bench variants (CGB, Spoto, Paused
  Larsen, comp bench when cleared), squat variants (Paused, tempo), pull variants (Paused
  RDL, RDL, deficit). 1–2 per day per block; swap at least one between blocks.
- **Tier 3 — Accessories** (partially rotate): rows, pulldowns, curls, triceps, hip
  abduction, leg curl, lateral raises, face pulls, cable work. Rotate ~50% per block;
  keep ≥1 carry-over accessory per slot for a baseline.
